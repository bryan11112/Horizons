About Us

Read about how our team came up with Talanton!

  • Chef's Dilemma?

    Cooking can be daunting for beginner chefs. Even with a recipe to follow, it can be quite time-consuming for beginners to identify and add precise amount of condiments.
    Furthermore, kitchen condiments often clutters up the kitchen space and create inconvenience for cooking, which can seriously hinder the learning progress.

  • Where Did We Get The Inspiration From?

    If you have ever observed a bartender at work, you will notice that the drink tap is a mighty tool.
    Not only can the user dispense different beverages with the drink tap at the touch of a button, the user can also fill up drinks at his/her own disposal with a comfortable amount of reach, thanks to the hand trigger and extendible cord.

  • Talanton's Born.

    Inspired by the bar tap, our team has designed a novel kitchen device that aims to simplify the cooking process for beginner chefs.
    Named after the Greek word for measurement, Talanton is a hand-held, cordless device that dispenses multiple powder-form condiments at precise user-requested servings.

Our product

Talanton is a hand-held, cordless device that dispenses multiple powder-form condiments at precise user-requested servings.

User-friendly Features

User Interface

  • Through the 1.8” colour LCD and buttons, users can navigate easily through a user-friendly menu to select the condiment type and weight that they would like to add from the four types of condiment.
  • Once programmed, Talanton will automatically dispense the condiments in the order of selection at the bottom of the device.

Dispensing Mechanism

  • Talanton dispenses condiments using a servo motor, which pushes each condiment channel down against a spring to open up a valve at the end, which releases the content from at the bottom of the device.
  • When a particular condiment is requested for, the servo can be rotated to four orientations by a precision motor to interact with a particular condiment channel for dispensing.

Measurement

  • To ensure that the condiment addition is accurate by weight, careful studies of flowrates for different condiments are carried out.
  • The internal profile and geometry of the condiment channels are optimised to prevent any clogging for the pre-defined powder condiments.

Food Safety

  • To meet food safety standards, condiments are stored inside individual sealed containers with anti-microbial surface coating to ensure the highest level of hygiene and prevent cross-contamination.
  • The humidity inside the containers is controlled to prevent any particle binding and bacterial growth.
  • Talanton is designed to be easily disassembled for periodic cleaning.

Aesthetics

  • Talanton uses a mixture of brushed aluminium and high gloss plastic surface which are good to the touch.
  • The two-tone colour scheme compliments well with modern kitchen settings.
  • Its lightweight design and dimensions similar to the average 500mL water bottle ensures the ease of use during operation.

User-friendly Features

  • Talanton is cordless and battery-powered so it is portable around the kitchen.
  • Side markers on the main body of the device indicate the content level in each condiment channel - each has a volume of 55 cm3.
  • A wall-mounted refill and recharging station allows the device to be used in extended period of time and helps to clean up precious cooking area.

Website Development


    1. What makes a good website?

      2. There are many factors that contribute to a good website. In my opinion, they can be divided into two areas – performance and design.

      Performance:

      1. Fast loading pages – Enhanced loading speed is achieved by compressing HTML, CSS and JavaScript and optimising image sizes.
      2. Easy navigation – A logical page hierarchy should be used with useful features such as clickable buttons. It should stick to the “three-click-rule” as closely as possible so that visitors can get the required information within three clicks.
      3. Flexible visuals – The visuals should be responsive and adaptive for all screen sizes.

      Design:

      1. Good understanding of typography - Readable and visually appealing fonts should be used with consistency in fonts, sizes, headings and button styles. Also, an appropriate use of keywords, meta-data, and other SEO-sensitive element is helpful to increase the readership of the website.
      2. Appropriate spacing – The website should not be overburdened with text and should have a good level of negative space to provide breathing room.
      3. Colours - A complementary colour palette can create a pleasing atmosphere and enhance the users’ experience.


    2. A description of accessibility and the considerations you have made for it.

      3. A website should accommodate to all types of audience. Accessibility can be achieved in many aspects of web design.

      First, the text content should be easy to understand without overlapping or conflicting information. The content should not be designed in a way that is known to cause medical conditions such as seizures.

      Second, the website should function in a predictable manner with logical operations to help users navigate, find content, and determine where they are on the webpage. All functionality should be available from a keyboard. For instance, on this website, the ‘Tell me more’ button on the homepage takes the reader directly to the ‘Our product’ section and circumvents the “About us” section, therefore there is a short description of the product at the section beginning to give the reader an overview of the product before learning about the product features.

      Third, the website should be perceivable by providing text alternatives for any non-text content or time-based media, such as text description in the form of large prints, speech, symbols or simple language. It will also be easier for users to see and hear the content if the foreground is separated from the background.

      Lastly, the website should be robust in the sense that it is compatible with current and future user agents, such as the use of assistive technologies.


    3. A description of GDPR and the considerations you have made (or should make) for privacy on your website.

      4. GDPR stands for General Data Protection Regulation. In effect from May 25, 2018, GDPR is a data privacy and protection regulation that provides protection of personal data and personally identifiable information for people living in the EU. There are specific obligations on the ‘controllers’ and ‘processors’ of personal data, such as the need to receive consent from a user before collecting and using their data. Major fines would be imposed if agents fail to be compliant.

      For GDPR compliance, there should be readily available information about the Privacy Policy from the footer of the webpage and a pop-up cookie consent message at the beginning of website browsing. The Privacy Policy should be very clear about the legal basis upon which the personal data that is being stored and processed. The website owner should only use the data for the purpose that the consent is given and store the data for the minimum amount of time.

      Also, the ‘Contact us’ form collects information such as the users’ name, email address, IP address and message. Therefore, there should be a tick box that requires the user to confirm that they have read and agreed to the Terms and Conditions and Privacy Policy (link provided) for the use and processing of data, and a tick box that checks for marketing permission through email contact. Furthermore, for the information processing of under 18’s, there should be systems in place to verify the users’ age and obtain parental or guardian consent for any data processing activity of individuals under the digital age.

      In addition, the data should be kept securely through the use of monitoring software to track and detect any attempts to hack the website or inject malware. In the event of a data breach, the Information Commissioner's Office (ICO) should be notified within 72 hours.


    4. What are the marketing steps you would take to drive people to your website?

      5. One of the most important steps to drive people to a website is to maximise the SEO (search engine optimisation) as this is a very effective and free way to attract readers from search engines. This is accomplished by setting certain keywords and phrases throughout the website, adding alternative text to images and optimising the headings.

      In addition, depending on the target audience of your website/product, social media is an effective channel for marketing because it is a large part of people’s everyday lives nowadays. Facebook, Twitter and Instagram are some of the platforms to consider for marketing. It raises awareness about the brand and the product and encourages content sharing by the audience, which is a form of free promotion. Interaction with the audience also helps to build the brand image. Other effective digital marketing tools include email marketing, guest blogging and forum advertisement, which can be further explored.


    5. What are the future steps you would take if given additional time? This should include steps required to expand and e-commerce.

      6. For future expansion, I will look to developing e-commerce in my website. To make sure that the website runs smoothly, the first thing I would do is to obtain an SSL certificate alongside my domain name. It provides important and secure communication between a visitor’s web browser and the web server hosting my website. In the near future, I would focus on targeting the UK market for my product, therefore I would select a UK-based server to optimise my website speed. Also, the hosting package for my website should take into consideration the uptime and performance, such as the ability to deal with extra traffic and the reliability of the host. Cloud hosting solutions would be considered for the increase in server capacity with demand.

      With e-commerce platforms, there are many options to choose from such as Saas (Software as a Service), off-the-shelf software and bespoke software. Although a bespoke software is costliest to set up, I will be able to optimise the setup and customise to fit my products, unlike Saas and off-the-shelf software that are heavily-templated and similar with one another. This is worth-investing in because changing platforms at a later date can be very time-consuming and expensive and e-commerce is a popular sales channel.

      Furthermore, I will set up an internet merchant account (IMA) to take money online and select a payment service provider (PSP). As there is credit card data storage and processing involved, it is crucial to meet the appropriate level of Payment Card Industry Security Standard Council (PCI SSC) compliance. For instance, regular vulnerability assessments should be completed against my website by a qualified security assessor to check for known vulnerabilities.
